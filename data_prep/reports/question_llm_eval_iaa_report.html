What is IAA? When several "annotators" (here, 4 LLMs) score the same items, inter-annotator agreement measures how often they agree, beyond what we'd expect by chance. High agreement means the rubric is unambiguous and the models interpret it similarly. Low agreement means the rubric is fuzzy or some models drift from it.

How to read these numbers.
  • % unanimous and % within ±1 are straightforward percentages of items: simple, intuitive, but they include agreement that could happen by chance alone (especially because the score distribution is skewed toward 1 and 2).
  • Fleiss' κ is reported on a conventional scale: 0 = chance-level agreement, 1 = perfect agreement. The "percentage" version (κ × 100) shown underneath is not a "% of items the models agreed on" — it's the fraction of the way from chance-level (0%) to perfect (100%). Rough interpretation guide: <20% slight, 20–40% fair, 40–60% moderate, 60–80% substantial, >80% almost perfect.
Fleiss' κ treats the four complexity values 0, 1, 2, 3 as unordered labels, so any disagreement counts equally — 0 vs 3 is treated as exactly as bad as 1 vs 2. That's an imperfect fit for a roughly-ordered scale like ours, but it's a reasonable choice given that the scale isn't a clean numeric ordinal either: the values were introduced by the rubric (data_prep/prompts/03_question_assessment.md) to label four qualitative categories (paraphrased: 0 = unanswerable, 1 = directly findable in text, 2 = needs simple inference, 3 = needs deeper synthesis), and category 0 ("unanswerable") is arguably not on the same axis at all. For finer-grained pairwise analysis that does account for distance on the scale, see the linear / quadratic weighted κ columns in the pairwise table (section 3).

3. Pairwise agreement — complexity_score

Exact %: how often two models gave the identical score.
Within-1 %: how often they agreed within ±1 (e.g. one said 2, the other 3). This is more lenient and useful for ordinal scales where neighbours matter less than distant disagreements.
Cohen's κ (kappa): agreement after subtracting the agreement you'd get just by random chance. 0 = chance-level. 1 = perfect. Rough guide: <0.2 slight, 0.2–0.4 fair, 0.4–0.6 moderate, 0.6–0.8 substantial, >0.8 almost perfect.
Linear / Quadratic weighted κ: penalises distant disagreements more than near ones (so 0 vs 3 hurts more than 0 vs 1). Quadratic weighting penalises distance even more strongly. For ordinal scores like ours, weighted κ is usually the most appropriate measure.
